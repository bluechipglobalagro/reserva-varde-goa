## 1. Cover page

**4BHK Signature Modular Luxury Forest Estate** — the flagship product of Reserva Verde Goa. A pavilion-style ground-level container estate set inside a private 1-acre eco-sensitive plantation, positioned in the Neturlim / Netravali forest-valley belt of South Goa.

---

## 2. Executive summary

A flagship modular eco-estate built from twelve 40 ft high-cube containers and one optional 20 ft staff module, organised as a three-wing pavilion plan around a 420 sq.ft central courtyard with a reflective stone-edged pool. The architecture is deliberately calm and horizontal: standing-seam metal roof, deep eaves, bamboo-batten rainscreen on container faces, charred-timber feature walls, laterite stone base course at 300 mm plinth. The plan separates three discrete arrival sequences — guest (south foyer), family (master court), service (east) — so guest, family and back-of-house circulation never cross.

The estate is the largest fixed product in the Reserva Verde Goa catalogue, designed for HNI families, retreat operators, hospitality-focused owners and lifestyle investors. It can operate as a primary residence, a flagship second home, or a private wellness retreat — and is engineered to accommodate optional managed plantation, farm-stay rental, and wellness retreat programmes subject to permissions.

## 3. Project context

Reserva Verde Goa is a **proposed 1000-acre eco-sensitive private estate destination** in South Goa, planned with approximately 900 private 1-acre modular eco estates. The site sits in the Neturlim / Netravali forest-valley region, surrounded by hills, waterfalls, the Netravali Wildlife Sanctuary landscape, the Bubbling Lake, the Salaulim Dam reservoir, and South Goa's quieter inland nature-tourism belt. Final location, boundary, access, and approvals are subject to ongoing survey and statutory verification.

## 5. Design philosophy

The 4BHK is conceived as **three connected pavilions framing a still water court** — not a container house, not a single villa block. The philosophy follows three principles:

1. **Horizontal calm.** Every volume sits on the ground at a single level. Roof lines are long, low-pitched, with deep eaves. The estate reads as a quiet horizon line set into the forest.
2. **Choreographed circulation.** Three discrete arrival sequences — guest, family, service — never intersect. Each wing has its own private threshold; the central courtyard is the only shared crossing.
3. **Materials that age well.** Bamboo battens patinate to silver-grey; lime plaster softens with monsoon; laterite stone holds its warmth in low light; charred timber (shou sugi ban) deepens with sun. Aluminium and steel sit hidden inside the modules. The building gains character with time.

The result is a private forest residence with the operational efficiency of modular construction, the spatial grace of pavilion architecture, and the eco discipline of a plantation estate.

## 6. 1-acre site planning strategy

The 1-acre plot is treated as a layered landscape: arrival → forecourt → home → courtyard → outdoor lifestyle → plantation → buffer. Sight-lines are framed twice — once from the gate (forecourt + entry foyer line-of-sight to the courtyard tree), once from the master suite (long view across the plantation to the western canopy).

The home occupies roughly 10% of the plot footprint; the plantation occupies ~50%; outdoor lifestyle and arrival take ~21%; water, utility and landscape buffer take the remainder. Service circulation is hedged into a discreet eastern spine that never enters the guest path.

## 11. Container module strategy

Twelve **40 ft high-cube ISO containers** (40 × 8 × 9.5 ft, 320 sq.ft each, ~8'10" clear interior height after insulation) form the primary habitable volumes. One **optional 20 ft module** (20 × 8 × 9.5 ft, 160 sq.ft) houses staff/caretaker quarters with a separate service entry and its own attached toilet, isolated to the eastern service spine.

Foundations are helical steel screw piles — zero concrete pour, zero excavation of mature tree roots, 450 mm underfloor air gap for monsoon ventilation. Each module is pre-fitted off-site (insulation, MEP rough-in, joinery, finishes) and craned into final position over a 3–4 day install window. End-of-life reversibility is preserved across the entire estate.

### How the modules connect

All twelve containers are linked through a **continuous insulated glass-link spine** that traces the perimeter of the central courtyard. The spine is a 2.2 m wide × 3 m tall curtain-wall corridor with 1,200 mm mullions and double-glazed low-E argon-filled units — primary circulation, daylight gallery, and SW monsoon cross-ventilation channel. Container-to-container joints are flashed and gasketed; floor levels unified with a 50 mm screed transition. The North, West and East wings each have one private threshold off the spine for residents who want to bypass the courtyard route.

## 13. Outdoor living plan

- **Private garden court (master)** — 200 sq.ft walled garden off the master suite, west-facing, fragrant planting (jasmine, frangipani, raat-rani), evening lighting.

- **Plantation-facing forest sit-out** — quiet hammock alcoves at three points along the looped walking trail.
