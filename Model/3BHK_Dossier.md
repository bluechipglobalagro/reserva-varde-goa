# 3BHK Premium Modular Agro Estate
**Reserva Verde Goa — Architectural Blueprint Dossier**

> Built-up: 3,500 sq.ft · Estate: 1 Acre · Ground-Level Only · 8 × 40 ft + 2 × 20 ft modules · L-Shape Pavilion + Central Courtyard

---

## 1. Design concept summary

An L-shape pavilion residence assembled from eight 40 ft high-cube containers and two 20 ft service modules — strictly single-storey, strictly ground-level — anchored around a 340 sq.ft open-to-sky biophilic courtyard at the inner corner of the L. The architecture reads as a contemporary tropical forest pavilion rather than a container house: bamboo-batten rainscreen on the exterior, charred-timber feature walls, laterite stone base course, lime-plaster end panels, standing-seam metal roof with deep overhangs and discreet integrated solar PV. The home opens fully to the plantation on its long inner edge and tucks its service back-of-house behind the kitchen wing.

The estate is sized for HNI families, NRIs, lifestyle investors, wellness buyers and farm-stay owners — the dual-program home accommodates everyday family living AND optional rental / retreat operation without compromising privacy.

---

## 2. 2D layout explanation

The plan is two long pavilions meeting at a glazed inner corner:

- **North wing — Bedrooms (private):** C-5 Master Suite → C-6 Master Bath/Wardrobe → C-7 Bedroom 2 → C-8 BR2 Bath/Connector Lounge → C-9 Bedroom 3 → C-10 BR3 Bath/Study/Family Lounge. Bedrooms face north-east for stable morning light, deep eaves shelter the long elevation, and each bedroom has direct access to a private deck pocket along the plantation edge.
- **South wing — Public (living + kitchen):** C-1 Entrance Foyer + Living extension → C-2 Main Living → C-3 Dining + Open Kitchen → C-4 Utility/Store/Powder service module. The public wing opens fully through floor-to-ceiling glass sliders onto the central courtyard and the long forest verandah.
- **Glass-link spine** runs along the inner edge of both wings — primary circulation, monsoon-wind ventilation channel, and visual frame for the courtyard.
- **Central courtyard** carries a stone water channel, native fern planting and a single canopy tree; it cools the home via stack-effect ventilation and brings daylight into every habitable room.
- **Forest deck + outdoor dining pergola** wrap the south-east corner facing the plantation.
- **Service spine** is a discreet, hedged pathway behind the kitchen wing — separate utility entry, no crossover with guest circulation.

---

## 3. 40 ft container module strategy

Eight **40 ft high-cube ISO containers** (40 × 8 × 9.5 ft, 320 sq.ft each, ~8'10" clear height after insulation) form the primary habitable volumes — column-free interiors with full corner-casting load capacity. Two **20 ft modules** (20 × 8 × 9.5 ft, 160 sq.ft each) absorb wet zones, utility, and connector lounge functions — concentrating plumbing risers, reducing wet-cell complexity, and giving the architectural composition a more nuanced silhouette than a single-volume bar.

Foundations are helical steel screw piles — zero concrete pour, zero excavation of mature tree roots, 450 mm underfloor air gap for monsoon ventilation. Modules are pre-fitted off-site (insulation, MEP, finishes, joinery) and craned into final position in two to three working days. End-of-life reversibility is preserved: every module is unbolted, unpiled and removable without site scarring.

## 7. Eco-sensitive specifications

**Construction & envelope**
- Helical steel screw-pile foundation — zero concrete pour, mature tree-root preservation
- 450 mm under-floor ventilation clearance for monsoon airflow
- Insulated walls (50 mm PIR + bamboo-batten rainscreen) and insulated roof (75 mm PIR)
- Anti-rust treatment for Goa coastal humidity — marine-grade epoxy coating on container shells, hot-dip galvanised connectors
- Natural cladding: bamboo battens (70%) + lime-plaster end panels + charred-timber (shou sugi ban) feature elevation
- Laterite stone base course at 300 mm plinth height
- Large shaded openings — operable timber-louvre screens on west elevation
- Termite-resistant landscape detailing (gravel skirt, sealed plinth, treated timber decking)
- Low-VOC paints, water-based oiled-timber sealers throughout
- Premium waterproofing — torch-applied membrane at roof penetrations, butyl tape at all junctions

**Roof**
- Solar-ready: structural capacity for 8–10 kWp PV array, conduits and inverter wall pre-engineered
- Heat-reflective standing-seam metal, high-SRI coating
- Multi-slope routing engineered to twin RWH tanks
- 900–1500 mm shaded overhangs + secondary pergola layer on verandah
- Optional green-roof patch over the family lounge module

**Water**
- Twin 12,500 L rainwater harvesting tanks (~25,000 L combined) at NE corner
- Dual groundwater recharge pits sized to plot percolation
- Greywater reed-bed filter pond feeding drip-irrigated herbal garden
- Twin-chamber bio-septic with planted leach field — no soak pit into groundwater
- Drip-irrigation grid for plantation, orchard, food forest, and herbal garden
- Low-flow dual-flush WCs, aerated taps throughout

**Energy**
- Solar-ready DC + AC architecture, inverter wall, net-meter provision
- LED throughout (3000 K interior / 2700 K outdoor)
- Cross-ventilation + courtyard stack-effect cooling + glass-spine wind tunnel
- 10 kWh battery bank provision (scalable)
- Energy-efficient low-E argon-filled DGU on E/W/S; single-glazed on shaded N for cost efficiency

**Landscape**
- Native canopy buffer along plot boundary — privacy + biodiversity corridor
- 55% plantation (buyer-selected — premium mix: mango / coconut / cashew / teak+pepper)
- Herbal + medicinal garden strip adjacent to kitchen
- Fruit-orchard accent zone (Alphonso / Mankurad / Hilsa)
- Butterfly + bird-friendly flowering native belt
- Permeable laterite + gravel walking paths — zero impermeable surface in plantation
- Natural stone pathways, plantation-facing deck on stilts
- On-site composting twin-chamber at utility yard
