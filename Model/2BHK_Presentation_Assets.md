## 4 Elevation concept prompts

Use these prompts in any AI image generator (Midjourney, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion XL) or hand them to your architectural illustrator.

### Front elevation (south face) — primary
> Architectural 2D front elevation of a single-storey ground-level modular container home, viewed from the south plantation side. Six 40 ft high-cube shipping containers arranged in a U-shape pavilion, with the entry foyer + grand living + dining + open kitchen presenting toward the viewer in a long horizontal composition. Bamboo-batten rainscreen cladding on container faces (vertical battens, 30 mm × 50 mm), laterite stone base course at 300 mm plinth, lime-plaster end panels, standing-seam metal roof (mid-grey) with a 1,200 mm overhang. Large floor-to-ceiling sliding glass doors (3 panels, 2,400 × 2,400 mm) opening onto a covered pergola verandah. Pivot teak entry door at centre-left with a charred-timber (shou sugi ban) feature wall. Discreet flush solar PV array on the roof slope. Warm concealed LED lighting at the plinth. Native canopy trees in the mid-distance, soft golden-hour light. Style: contemporary tropical luxury, calm, premium, eco-sensitive. Architectural drawing line-weights, 1:100 scale annotation. Palette: forest green, ivory, muted gold, natural beige, charcoal labels. No 3D shading on the elevation itself — strict orthographic line drawing with material hatching.

### Rear elevation (north face) — bedroom side

The inner courtyard reveal is visible through the U opening. Privacy screen of timber slats set 1,500 mm from the elevation, 1,800 mm tall. Deep north-face eaves (900 mm). Standing-seam roof + solar PV array visible from this angle. Bamboo cladding pattern continues. Style: contemporary tropical, restrained, line-drawing with material hatching. 1:100 scale.

### Left side elevation (west) — master suite side

### Right side elevation (east) — kitchen / utility side

### Long description (500–700 chars — for model detail page)

> The 2BHK Compact Modular Eco Estate is the entry-tier product of the Reserve Varde Goa masterplan — a single-storey U-shape pavilion residence assembled from six 40 ft high-cube containers and one 20 ft service module, organised around an 8 × 5 m open-to-sky biophilic courtyard at its heart. The home is strictly ground-level, with bamboo-batten cladding, charred-timber feature walls, laterite stone base course, and a standing-seam metal roof carrying integrated solar PV. The 1-acre estate places the home centrally with 60% plantation belt, an herbal garden adjacent to the kitchen, a forest-facing deck with optional plunge pool, and a walking trail loop through the orchard. Designed for couples, NRIs, wellness buyers, and farm-stay owners — premium without being ostentatious, eco-sensitive without compromising luxury.

## How to use this asset pack on the website

1. **Hero image** on the 2BHK model detail page → use `2BHK_Presentation_Combined.svg` as the wide hero banner
2. **Inside the dossier modal Blueprint tab** → replace the ASCII drawing pane with `2BHK_FloorPlan_2D.svg` inline
3. **Inside the dossier modal Schedules tab** → reference `2BHK_FloorPlan_2D.svg` as a "View 2D plan" link beside the room schedule
4. **Inside the dossier modal CAD tab** → reference all three SVG files as downloads + the prompts above as copy buttons
5. **On the model card thumbnail** → use a small crop of `2BHK_SitePlan_1Acre.svg` as the card image
6. **In the investor deck (PDF)** → embed `2BHK_Presentation_Combined.svg` at A3 landscape; one slide per model

## What's NOT in this pack (and why)

| Not included | Why |
|---|---|
| Photoreal renders (JPG/PNG) | Renders need to be produced by a 3D visualiser using the four prompts above. The prompts are tuned for Midjourney / SDXL / commissioned Enscape work. |
| AutoCAD DWG files | DWG is platform-specific — the SVG floor plan can be imported into AutoCAD, but native DWG production requires an architect with a CAD seat. The 2BHK_CAD_Brief.md gives the architect everything they need to draft the DWG. |
| Furniture detail / interior styling renders | Interior styling is a separate scope — best handled by an interior designer working from the floor plan + render prompts. |
| 3D BIM model | A BIM (Revit) model is a separate workstream from this 2D pack. The SketchUp prompt in the CAD brief is the starter point for a 3D model. |
