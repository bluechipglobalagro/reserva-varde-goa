# 2BHK Compact Modular Eco Estate
## Complete 2D CAD Drafting Brief — Reserva Verde Goa

> **Built-up:** 2,500 sq.ft · **Estate:** 1 Acre · **Ground-level only** · **6 × 40 ft HC + 1 × 20 ft modules** · **U-shape courtyard plan**
>
> A premium ₹2000 Cr-level private eco-jungle estate concept for HNI buyers, NRIs, investors, wellness retreat users, and farm-stay owners. This document is the complete drafter handover for a 26-sheet CAD drawing package.

---

## 1. Design concept summary

A premium ground-level modular forest estate built from six 40 ft high-cube ISO containers and one 20 ft service module, arranged in a U-shape pavilion plan around a central open-to-sky biophilic courtyard. Strictly single-storey, strictly ground-level — no stacking, no vertical assembly, no hotel-pod typology. The architecture reads as a contemporary tropical forest pavilion — bamboo-batten rainscreen on container faces, charred-timber feature wall on the entry elevation, laterite stone base course at 300 mm plinth, lime-plaster end panels, standing-seam metal roof with deep eaves and integrated solar PV.

Design language references: Aman forest retreat × Six Senses eco-luxury × Goan jungle villa × modern modular architecture × forest-facing private estate. The home is sized and engineered for a couple or small family with optional rental / farm-stay readiness.

## 2. 2BHK 2,500 sq.ft planning logic

The 2,500 sq.ft is built from three layers stacked horizontally on a single ground plane:

| Layer | Area (sq.ft) | What sits here |
|---|---:|---|
| **Container interiors** (6 × 40 ft HC + 1 × 20 ft) | ~2,080 | Living, Dining, Kitchen, Master suite, BR2 suite, Study, Utility, Store, Powder |
| **Insulated glass-link connectors** | ~180 | Glass spine that ties the U together |
| **Roofed verandah + courtyard-facing covered transition spaces** | ~240 | South verandah + plantation-deck overlap + courtyard-edge sit-out |
| **Total built-up** | **~2,500** | ±2% architectural tolerance |

The U-shape opens to the south plantation, with the courtyard sitting at the geometric centre. Cross-ventilation pulls SW monsoon air through the glass spine; deep north-east eaves shelter the bedroom wing; the kitchen has a separate service entry hidden behind a hedged side yard.

## 3. Recommended layout type

Three layouts compared:

| Option | Pros | Cons | Score |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Linear Forest Villa | Simple construction; easy services; long forest-facing deck | Less premium identity; no internal courtyard | 6.5 |
| 2. L-Shape Forest Container Home | Better privacy; strong indoor-outdoor; good plantation-facing deck | No protected courtyard centre | 8.0 |
| **3. U-Shape Courtyard Container Home** ✓ | Most premium estate identity; protected open-to-sky courtyard; strongest retreat feel; cross-ventilation excellent; rental-ready | Slightly higher construction logistics (mitigated by linear glass spine) | **9.2** |

**Recommended: Option 3 — U-Shape Courtyard.** The courtyard is the architectural and emotional centre of the home. It cools the building via stack effect, brings daylight into every habitable room, and gives the property a signature aerial read. For a 2BHK at 2,500 sq.ft the U-shape is the sweet spot: large enough for a courtyard, small enough to remain efficient and intimate.

## 7. Container module strategy

**Modules used:** 6 × 40 ft HC + 1 × 20 ft service module = **7 containers total**

| Module type | Size | Internal area | Use |
|---|---|---:|---|
| 40 ft HC ISO container | 40' × 8' × 9.5' (12.19 × 2.44 × 2.90 m) | 320 sq.ft | Primary habitable volumes — living, dining, kitchen, master, BR2 |
| 20 ft ISO container | 20' × 8' × 9.5' (6.10 × 2.44 × 2.90 m) | 160 sq.ft | Service module — utility + store |
| Insulated DGU glass corridor | 2,200 mm × 3,000 mm × variable length | 180 sq.ft total | Glass spine linking modules |
| Roofed pergola verandah | Variable | 200 sq.ft | Covered south verandah |

**How the 2,500 sq.ft is built up:**
- 6 × 40 ft container interiors: 6 × 320 = 1,920 sq.ft
- 1 × 20 ft container interior: 160 sq.ft
- Glass connector corridors: 180 sq.ft (enclosed, climate-controlled)
- Enclosed verandah + courtyard transition: 240 sq.ft (roofed, partially enclosed)
- **TOTAL BUILT-UP: ~2,500 sq.ft**

Foundations: helical steel screw piles — zero concrete pour, mature tree-root preservation, 450 mm underfloor air gap for monsoon ventilation. Modules pre-fitted off-site (insulation + MEP + finishes) and craned into final position over 2 working days.

**How modules connect:** All seven containers link through a continuous insulated **glass-link spine** running along the courtyard's inner edge. The spine is a 2,200 mm wide × 3,000 mm tall curtain-wall corridor with 1,200 mm mullion spacing, double-glazed low-E argon-filled units. Container-to-container joints are flashed and gasketed; floor levels unified with a 50 mm screed transition.

## 20. AutoCAD drafter instructions

Step-by-step drafting workflow:

1. **Setup**: open new drawing in metric units (mm); set Limits to 65,000 × 65,000 mm; set drawing scale 1:100 (model space), plotted A1.
2. **Layers**: create all layers per Section 18 with assigned colours and lineweights.
3. **Title block**: insert RV title-block template at all 26 sheet tabs.
4. **Plot boundary** (Sheet 02): draw 63,400 × 63,400 mm rectangle on A-SITE.
5. **Container modules** (Sheet 04, 05): draw 6 × (12,200 × 2,440 mm) rectangles for C-1 to C-6 and 1 × (6,100 × 2,440 mm) for C-7 on A-CONTAINER. Place in U-shape with 4,500 mm courtyard between the two long arms; orient long axis E-W for C-1 and C-2; orient long axis N-S for C-3 to C-6.
6. **Walls and partitions** (Sheet 04): draw all walls on A-WALL; wall thickness 100 mm external (with insulation), 50 mm internal.
7. **Glass-link spine**: draw curtain-wall corridor 2,200 mm wide × variable length on A-CONTAINER (hatched with glazing pattern).
8. **Courtyard**: draw 8 × 5 m rectangle on A-COURTYARD; add stone water channel 3 × 1.2 m within.
9. **Doors and windows**: insert RV-DOOR and RV-WINDOW blocks at every opening; tag with codes from Sheet 20 schedule.
10. **Furniture**: import RV-FURN block library (sofa, dining, beds, wardrobes); place on A-FURN at 0.5 pt strokes.
11. **Plantation hatch** (Sheet 02, 23): hatch plantation zone with ANSI31 @ 1,500 mm scale, rotation 45°, on A-PLANTATION.
12. **Utility symbols** (Sheet 24): insert RV-UTIL block library (RWH, septic, composter, solar shed, EV).
13. **Dimensions** (Sheet 07): use DIMSTYLE "RV-DIM"; text 2.5 mm; arrowhead 2.5 mm; extension lines offset 1.5 mm.
14. **Text styles**: RV-TITLE (5 mm, font: serif), RV-NOTE (2.5 mm, font: sans), RV-ROOM (2.5 mm, uppercase, letter-spacing 0.5).
15. **Sections**: cut Sections A-A and B-B; show on Sheets 18 and 19 at 1:50.
16. **Elevations**: cut elevations from floor plan with VIEW commands; render with cladding hatch on A-HATCH.
17. **Save**: DWG 2018 format; plot using RV-MONO.ctb for line weights; export PDF at A1 for each sheet.
18. **QA**: verify every dimension closes (sum of internal dims = external); verify every door has a tag; verify every fixture has an electrical / plumbing point.
