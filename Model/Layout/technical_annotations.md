## 📐 1. Architectural & Dimensional Framework

The modular architecture follows a precise grid designed to keep the container structural integrity intact without complex cuts:

* **Primary Modules (C1 – C6):** Six 40ft High Cube shipping containers.
  - *Standard Dimensions:* $12,200\text{ mm} \times 2,440\text{ mm} \times 2,900\text{ mm}$ (Length $\times$ Width $\times$ Height).
  - *Placement:* Strict ground-level layout (Z-plane only). No vertical stacking to minimize wind loads in the Goan jungle.
* **Service Module (C7):** One 20ft Standard shipping container, completely detached to prevent utility vibration/noise transfer.
  - *Standard Dimensions:* $6,100\text{ mm} \times 2,440\text{ mm} \times 2,900\text{ mm}$.
* **Plinth Base:** Raised concrete slab finished in native laterite stone masonry.
  - *Elevation:* Z = $0\text{ mm}$ to Z = $450\text{ mm}$. This provides critical protection against monsoonal surface runoff and soil splashback.
* **Roof Overhangs:** Deep horizontal canopy structures with solid charcoal fascia.
  - *Projection:* $1,000\text{ mm}$ (1.0m) past the container boundaries to provide shade and monsoon protection.

## 🌧️ 3. Monsoon & Heavy Rain Detailing

Designed specifically for heavy South Goa monsoonal downpours:

* **Concealed Edge Gutters:** $100\text{mm} \times 80\text{mm}$ bronze-colored collection channels modeled along the courtyard roof overhangs and the glass connector roof edges.
* **Heavy Downpipes:** Four vertical bronze $80\text{mm}$ diameter downpipes located at the courtyard corners to quickly drain water to the plinth harvesting channel.
* **Drip Edges:** A physical $40\text{mm}$ downward drip trim modeled along the underside of all roof overhangs to force rainwater to drop clear of the vertical cladding.
* **Glazed Walkway Junction Flashing:** Heavy vertical bronze flashing bands ($30\text{mm}$ thick) modeled at the junctions where the glass walkway meets container C4 (West wing) and C6 (East wing). These represent waterproof expansion joints.
* **Covered Entrance:** Flat roof canopy projecting over the entry deck to ensure a dry transition from the gravel driveway.

---

## ☀️ 4. Low-Profile Fixed Solar Array

Designed to resist high wind loads and monsoonal cyclonic forces:

* **PV Modules:** 6 detailed monocrystalline solar panels ($2,000\text{ mm} \times 1,000\text{ mm}$) on the West Wing roof (C3/C4).
* **Low-Angle Brackets:** Avoided high tilted steel frame racks. Instead, panels sit on two parallel low-profile mounting rails.
* **Slight Tilt:** Panels are tilted at a **$1.2^{\circ}$ slope (2% grade)**, dropping from Z = $3660\text{mm}$ (North) to Z = $3630\text{mm}$ (South). This provides:
  - Low wind lift coefficient during tropical depressions.
  - Sufficient angle for self-cleaning/rainwater runoff to prevent water pooling on panel glass.

## ⚙️ 5. Landscaped Utility & Service Zone

To support the self-sustaining nature of the villa without compromising premium aesthetics, a dedicated service zone is located on the East boundary (X=14000, Y=3000 to 7500):

* **STP / Bio-Digester Box:** A $1.5\text{m} \times 1.5\text{m} \times 1.1\text{m}$ charcoal box with bronze-finished inspection lids representing a modular decentralized blackwater bio-digester.
* **Rainwater Harvesting Tank:** A circular, large cylindrical harvesting tank with a finished metal lid, positioned near C7.
* **Greywater Reuse collection tank:** A dedicated reuse tank connected to the plinth and courtyard overflow channel.
* **Concealed Timber Privacy Screen:** A vertical timber slat privacy screen ($2.2\text{m}$ tall) running in front of the service zone to completely screen the machinery from the main entryway.

## 📝 6. Verification Requirements Checklist (Prior to Construction)

The local engineering team must verify the following:

1. **Soil & Pile Foundations:** Laterite base requires pile foundation checks to ensure no differential settlement between C1–C6 and C7 in soft forest soil.
2. **Gutters & Downpipe Sizing:** Peak rainfall calculations in Goa (typically $100\text{mm/hr}$) must be checked against the $80\text{mm}$ downpipe capacity.
3. **Flashing & Weather Seals:** Elastomeric seals at container-to-glass walkways must support the different thermal expansion rates of steel and glass.
4. **Solar Wind Uplift:** Low-profile brackets must be anchored directly into the shipping container's structural corner castings or reinforced roof ribs.
5. **STP Discharge Approvals:** Local Goan panchayat and environmental clearances for bio-digester discharge / graywater irrigation reuse.
