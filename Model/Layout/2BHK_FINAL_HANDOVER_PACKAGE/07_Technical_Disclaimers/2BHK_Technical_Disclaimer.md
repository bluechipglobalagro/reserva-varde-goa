## 🛠️ Required Local Verifications Prior to Construction

To ensure structural safety, environmental compliance, and structural performance under South Goa’s specific climatic parameters, local professional consultants must evaluate the following core engineering domains:

### 1. Soil Dynamics & Structural Foundations
* **Model Representation:** The conceptual model sits on a $450\text{mm}$ raised plinth resting on basic concrete perimeter piles.
* **Local Verification Required:** 
  - A registered civil/geotechnical engineer must perform core-drilling soil tests across the specific 1-acre plot to determine the bearing capacity of the forest subsoil.
  - Soft, clay-heavy, or high-organic jungle soils will require custom deep pile designs, concrete pad spacing, or structural grade beams to support the heavy loads of 7 steel container modules (exceeding 30 metric tons of dead load when fully loaded).
  - Foundation designs must be engineered to resist tropical soil expansion/contraction and prevent differential settlement across C1–C6 and C7.

### 2. Tropical Rainfall & Site Drainage (Monsoon Detailing)
* **Model Representation:** Conceptual gutters ($100\text{mm} \times 80\text{mm}$ channels), four $80\text{mm}$ downpipes, and a $200\text{mm}$ courtyard water reflecting channel are modeled.
* **Local Verification Required:**
  - A local MEP/hydrology engineer must recalculate these gutter and downpipe profiles using peak Goan monsoonal precipitation data ( Goa often experiences localized rainfall intensities exceeding $100\text{mm}$ per hour).
  - Downpipe diameters and gutter slopes must be calculated using sizing formulas (e.g., the Manning formula) to prevent overflowing and water backup under the roof canopy.
  - The concrete courtyard paving, reflecting channel, and plunge pool overflow must connect to a gravity-fed site storm drainage network that safely directs overflow away from the plinth foundation without causing erosion on the 1-acre forest plot.

### 3. Solar Wind Load & Cyclonic Uplift
* **Model Representation:** 6 PV panels mounted on fixed, low-profile rails, sloped at a gentle $1.2^{\circ}$ grade.
* **Local Verification Required:**
  - Goa resides in a high-wind speed cyclonic zone (Wind Zone 1, basic wind speed up to 33 m/s or 118 km/h).
  - A licensed structural engineer must design the physical anchoring brackets connecting the solar mounting rails directly into the shipping container's primary corner castings or structural roof ribs.
  - The structural stability of the container roof sheets must be assessed to ensure they can support the dead weight of the solar panels and the localized wind lift forces without oil-canning or tearing.

### 4. Decentralized Waste STP & Environmental Approvals
* **Model Representation:** A $1.5\text{m} \times 1.5\text{m} \times 1.1\text{m}$ decentralized STP/bio-digester service box and collection tanks are shown conceptually.
* **Local Verification Required:**
  - A certified sanitary engineer must calculate the active bio-digester tank volume based on the occupancy rate of the 2BHK villa (typically 4–6 adults).
  - On-site greywater filtration and irrigation discharge layouts must comply with local Goan panchayat guidelines, Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) wastewater standards, and forest conservation zone regulations.
  - The plumbing routes, venting, sludge clean-out ports, and plunge pool backwash/overflow integration must be designed by local mechanical and plumbing designers.

### 5. Mechanical & Thermal Insulation (HVAC)
* **Model Representation:** Corten shipping steel shells insulated with standard panels and glazed walkways are shown.
* **Local Verification Required:**
  - Steel shipping containers are highly conductive structures that act as extreme heat sinks in Goa’s hot and humid tropical climate.
  - A certified HVAC and building envelope consultant must design the insulation system (suggested polyurethane foam or high-density rock wool), vapor barriers, and passive ventilation air cavities.
  - Double-glazed units must meet local wind load parameters and thermal transmission limits (U-value and SHGC) to ensure comfort and efficiency.
