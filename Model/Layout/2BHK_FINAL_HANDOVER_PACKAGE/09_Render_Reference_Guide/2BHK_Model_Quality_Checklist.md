## 2. Real-World Engineering Clearances & Verification
Before translating this conceptual presentation model into physical construction on-site in South Goa, the local architect of record and engineering teams must certify and clear the following calculations:

### A. Structural Roof & Wind Load (Wind Speed Resistance)
* **Design Standard:** BIS IS-875 (Part 3) Wind Loads for India (Zone IV - South Goa coastal regions).
* **Wind Rating Required:** The structural steel container frames, roof systems, and low-profile solar racks must resist wind speeds up to **50 m/s (180 km/h)**, representing intense monsoonal squalls and cyclonic depressional winds.
* **Mounting Verification:** 
  * The solar panels must be directly bolted to horizontal heavy-gauge steel support rails.
  * The rails must be welded/bolted directly into the top corner castings and structural top headers of container wings C3 and C4.
  * The structural framing of the container shells must be verified to prevent wind-induced uplift and localized panel buckling.

### B. Monsoon Rain Drainage & Flow Math
* **Rainfall Intensity Standard:** Prepared for South Goa's peak monsoonal downpours (up to **100 mm/hour** precipitation density).
* **Roof Flow Calculations:**
  * **Catchment Area:** Total roof surface is approximately $208\text{ m}^2$ (comprising the South, West, and East wings).
  * **Peak Water Volume:** $208\text{ m}^2 \times 0.10\text{ m/hour} = 20.8\text{ m}^3\text{/hour}$ (approx. $347\text{ Liters/minute}$ total runoff).
* **System Sizing Verification:**
  * **Gutters:** The continuous $100\text{ mm}$ bronze gutters must have a minimum sloped grade of **1:100 (1%)** feeding the corner outlets.
  * **Downpipes:** The four $80\text{ mm}$ downpipes provide a combined discharge capacity of up to **420 Liters/minute** (using gravity flow head), which easily handles peak monsoonal downpours with a 20% safety factor.
  * **Plinth base drainage swales:** Gravel drainage trenches must lead to localized soak pits or bioswales with positive gravity slopes to prevent plinth water splashback and foundation erosion.

### C. Foundation Plinths & Laterite Retaining Masonry
* **Plinth Height:** The raised **450 mm plinth height** is legally required to lift the container modular floor frames above potential groundwater capillary rising zones and heavy rainwater splash heights.
* **Retaining Wall Math:**
  * Retaining stone walls ($300\text{ mm}$ thick) must be constructed with weep holes spaced every $1.2\text{ m}$ to relieve hydrostatic pressure behind the terraces during intense saturated soil events.
  * All container foundation piles (bored cast-in-situ concrete piles) must be anchored into stable soil strata beneath the organic humus layers.

### D. Bio-Digester STP & Ecological Systems
* **Sewage Treatment Plant Sizing:**
  * Designed for a maximum occupancy of 6 residents (including guests).
  * Standard Indian water consumption: $135\text{ Liters/capita/day (LCPD)}$.
  * Peak Daily Flow: $6 \times 135 = 810\text{ Liters/day}$.
* **STP Capacity Sizing:**
  * The decentralized Bio-Digester STP unit must have a minimal capacity of **1,200 Liters/day**, running three-chamber anaerobic decomposition.
  * Effluent water must be piped directly to subsurface irrigation lines feeding the bamboo boundary clusters (greywater reuse system).
* **Rainwater Harvesting Cistern:**
  * The $1,800\text{ mm}$ cylinder cistern must hold a minimum volume of **10,000 Liters**, equipped with a primary leaf trap/first-flush filtration system.

### E. Local Goan Municipal & Forest Clearances
* **CRZ (Coastal Regulation Zone) Audit:** Ensure the site is outside restricted coastal boundaries or no-development zones.
* **Goa Forest Department Clearance:** Confirm that none of the heavy perimeter jungle canopy or coconut palms modeled are classified as protected local species requiring cutting permits.
* **Panchayat Building Sanctions:** The structural modular container design, septic alignments, and setbacks must comply with local Village Panchayat regulations and fire-safety access requirements.
