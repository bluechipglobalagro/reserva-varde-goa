## 2. Multi-Level Site & Land Contours Plan
To eliminate the cartoonish "flat green lawn" and reflect the authentic, dramatic topography of a South Goa forest plot, the landscape was divided into three structural terraces, supported by heavy, textured retaining walls:

* **Low Terrace (Front Approach & Parking):** Modeled at **Z = -200 mm**. This serves as the primary arrival court. It hosts a natural winding gravel driveway, twin heavy laterite masonry gate piers, and a sliding timber welcome gate.
* **Mid Terrace (Villa Footprint Zone):** Modeled at **Z = 0 mm**. The entire 7-container modular villa structure sits on this level.
* **High Terrace (Rear Forest buffer):** Modeled at **Z = +250 mm**. This forms a lush, wild jungle backdrop. It features the private meditation firepit zone and the thick background jungle forest canopy.
* **Retaining Terraces Wall Details:**
  * **Low-to-Mid Edge:** A robust **300 mm thick Laterite Stone wall** running from Z = -200 mm to Z = +50 mm, preventing soil erosion and creating immediate architectural depth.
  * **Mid-to-High Edge:** A corresponding **300 mm Laterite Wall** running from Z = 0 mm to Z = +300 mm, acting as an organic barrier between the manicured courtyard and the wild rear canopy.

## 3. High-Fidelity Architectural Enhancements
The container volumes were upgraded from simple block massing into highly expressive modular structures:

* **Fine Timber Facade Striping:** We programmatically drew fine vertical teak wood slats ($40\text{ mm}$ wide, $10\text{ mm}$ deep, spaced at $150\text{ mm}$) over the exterior walls (bamboo and charred timber panels). This creates real, highly detailed shadow lines in SketchUp's graphics engine, giving the building a premium "Sugi-ban & Teak" resort finish.
* **Matte Charcoal Metal Columns & Headers:** All 12 corners and main structural borders of the container shells are framed with deep Slate Charcoal headers ($150 \times 150\text{ mm}$ steel boxes). This exposes the industrial structural frame of the container, creating a beautiful aesthetic tension with the soft wood and plaster.
* **Minimalist Bronze Window System:** Large glazing structures, sliding sliding-doors, and connector corridors are detailed with thin, anodized bronze frame lines ($40\text{ mm}$ profile). 
* **Raised Plinths:** Replaced simple floor heights with a beautiful, heavy **Laterite Stone base block (Z = 0 to 450 mm)** on all container wings, lifting the villa off the forest floor.

## 4. Advanced Monsoonal Protection Detail
To satisfy the strict "South Goa Monsoon Detailing" criteria, the upgraded model implements clear, visible water runoff and safety systems:

1. **Continuous Gutter Systems:** Custom $100\text{ mm}$ wide anodized bronze gutters are modeled along the inner roof eaves of the courtyard wings, running the full length of the double-glazed corridors.
2. **Courtyard Downpipes:** Four vertical $80\text{ mm}$ bronze downpipes are placed at each corner of the central courtyard, visibly connecting the roof gutters to the plinth base.
3. **Monsoon Safety Gravel Strip:** A continuous $300\text{ mm}$ wide, $20\text{ mm}$ deep river gravel drainage strip (`m_gravel`) is modeled directly at the base of the laterite plinths, designed to absorb roof splatter and runoff.
4. **Storm-Safe Panel Mounting:** The 6 solar panels on the West Wing roof are detailed with low-profile, solid bronze mounting frames, sloped at a gentle $1.2^\circ$ (2% grade) for rapid storm runoff while keeping a clean, low roof silhouette.
