### 2BHK Architectural Concept Summary
The Canopy Pavilion represents a paradigm shift in tropical estate living. Built upon a precise U-shaped footprint using seven structural steel shipping containers, the villa frames a private, symmetrical open-to-sky central courtyard. By placing all modules strictly on a single ground-level plane, the architecture blends seamlessly into the forest floor, eliminating vertical wind hazards while maximizing direct contact with nature. Floor-to-ceiling double-insulated structural glazing in heavy bronze frames replaces solid walls, offering a continuous, widescreen view of the pristine jungle context.

## 🌧️ 5. Climate-Ready & Ecological Engineering

### Monsoon-Ready Structural Detailing
* **Concealed Edge Gutters:** $100\text{mm} \times 80\text{mm}$ bronze-finished collection channels integrated along all roof overhangs and glazed connector corridors to handle tropical downpours.
* **Volumetric Drip Edges:** A physical $40\text{mm}$ drop trim under the roof edge to force rainwater to channel away from vertical walls.
* **Junction Flashings:** $30\text{mm}$ bronze-coated metal flashing plates installed at glass-to-steel container joints to provide elastic waterproof seals.
* **Elevated Plinth Base:** The entire modular system is set upon a $450\text{mm}$ raised concrete slab finished in local laterite stone, avoiding ground water splashes and mud tracking.

### Self-Sustaining Utilities Concept
* **Low-Profile Fixed Solar Array:** 6 PV panels mounted on fixed low-profile rails on the West wing roof, sloped at a gentle $1.2^{\circ}$ (2% grade) for optimal South wind-shedding and self-cleaning water runoff during heavy monsoons.
* **Rainwater Harvesting Cistern:** A detailed rainwater collection tank positioned near C7 to collect roof runoff for filtration and irrigation reuse.
* **Decentralized STP / Bio-Digester:** A hidden, landscaped bio-digester utility box that naturally processes blackwater on-site, recycling greywater for garden irrigation.
