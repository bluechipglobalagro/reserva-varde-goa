## Slide 4: Engineering — Monsoon Resilience & Self-Sufficiency
* **Slide Title:** "Zero-Carbon Off-Grid Infrastructure"
* **Visual Cue:** Elevated perspective focusing on the sloped roofs, solar arrays, and utility pads (`reserva_varde_2bhk_lumion_roof_solar.png`).
* **Slide Bullets:**
  * **Wind-Safe Solar Grid:** 6 monocrystalline panels ($3.3\text{ kWp}$) sloped at a low $1.2^\circ$ to prevent cyclonic wind-lift under BIS IS-875 standards.
  * **Monsoon Runoff Management:** Continuous $100\text{mm}$ bronze gutters and four $80\text{mm}$ downpipes designed to discharge peak Goan downpours ($347\text{ Liters/minute}$).
  * **Gravel Drainage Channels:** $300\text{mm}$ gravel strips surrounding plinths to prevent soil erosion and mud splatter.
  * **Decentralized STP:** $1,200\text{ L/day}$ Bio-Digester STP unit completely hidden behind vertical timber slat screen structures.
* **Speaker Notes:**  
  "South Goa experiences intense monsoonal weather, which represents a massive structural risk for standard developers. Reserva Varde Goa is engineered for absolute resilience. The solar arrays are sloped at a low $1.2^\circ$ directly into container structural steel headers, preventing wind-lift under 180 km/h gusts. The roof drainage handles peak rainfall intensities, while continuous gravel strips absorb splashback, maintaining clean teak facades throughout the wet season."
