# 2BHK Compact Luxury Modular Eco Estate
**Reserva Verde Goa — Architectural Blueprint Dossier**

> Built-up: 2,500 sq.ft · Estate: 1 Acre · Ground-Level Only · 5 × 40 ft + 3 × 20 ft modules

---

## 1. Design concept summary

A horizontal, single-storey U-shape forest courtyard residence assembled from five 40 ft high-cube containers and three 20 ft service modules. The architecture is deliberately low-slung — no stacking, no vertical container assemblies, no hotel-pod aesthetic — and reads as a contemporary tropical eco-pavilion rather than a container house. The U-shape frames a 260 sq.ft open-to-sky biophilic courtyard at the heart of the home, creating a microclimate-cooled core that pulls cross-ventilation through every habitable room and visually anchors the public, private, and service wings.

The estate is sized for couples, small families, NRIs, and eco-conscious second-home buyers who want a low-maintenance forest retreat with optional rental readiness. Cladding combines bamboo battens, lime plaster, and a single charred-timber feature elevation; the standing-seam metal roof carries 5–7 kWp of solar PV capacity and routes monsoon water into a 15,000 L rainwater harvesting sump.

---

## 2. 2D layout explanation

The plan reads as three connected pavilions:

- **West public wing** — Living Pavilion (C-1) flowing into Dining/Kitchen (C-3) with a connecting Lounge module (C-2), all opening onto the forest verandah and plantation deck.
- **East private wing** — Master Suite (C-5) and Bedroom 2 (C-7), each with its own attached bath module (C-6, C-8). Bedrooms face north-east for stable morning light and quiet plantation views.
- **North service bridge** — Utility/Storage (C-4) tucks behind the kitchen with a separate side-yard service entry, keeping back-of-house circulation completely off the guest paths.

All wings link through fully glazed, insulated glass corridors (Links 1–7) that double as circulation and as a solar/monsoon-tempered ventilation gallery. The covered forest verandah wraps the south face of the public wing; the plantation-facing deck extends east off the bedrooms.

---

## 3. 40 ft container module strategy

Five **40 ft high-cube ISO containers** (40 × 8 × 9.5 ft, 320 sq.ft each) form the primary habitable volumes — column-free interiors with ~8'10" clear ceiling height after insulation. Three **20 ft modules** (20 × 8 × 9.5 ft, 160 sq.ft each) absorb wet zones, utility, and connector functions, concentrating plumbing risers and reducing wet-cell complexity.

Foundations are helical steel screw piles — zero concrete pour, zero excavation of mature tree roots, 450 mm underfloor air gap for monsoon-season ventilation. Modules are pre-fitted off-site (insulation, MEP, finishes) and craned into final position in two working days. End-of-life reversibility is preserved: every module is unbolted, unpiled, and removable without site scarring.

## 6. 1-acre site placement plan

The home is **flexibly placed within the 1-acre boundary** — final position chosen per plot during site walk, factoring road access, view corridors, slope, water flow lines, sun path, prevailing SW monsoon wind, mature-tree preservation, and Vaastu-friendly orientation where feasible.

**Five recommended placement variants** (buyer-selected at design intake):

1. **Front-to-centre** — 25–30 ft setback from gate. Easiest access. Plantation wraps the rear and one side. *Default for first-phase buyers.*
2. **Centre** — full plantation buffer all around. Premium estate feel. Best long-term resale read.
3. **Side** — home shifts to one boundary, consolidates plantation as a single deep zone. Best for plantation-focused buyers.
4. **Rear** — home tucked at back, full plantation frontage between home and gate, maximum privacy.
5. **Courtyard-centric** — home wraps the central court with plantation on all four sides, resort-style.

**Default zoning (front-to-centre, indicative)**:

| Zone | Allocation | % of plot |
|---|---:|---:|
| Entry & arrival (driveway, parking, gate) | 2,200 sq.ft | 5.0% |
| Container home built-up | 2,500 sq.ft | 5.7% |
| Outdoor lifestyle (deck, sit-out, lawn) | 3,800 sq.ft | 8.7% |
| Plantation zone | 27,500 sq.ft | 63.1% |
| Water & sustainability (RWH, recharge, septic) | 1,800 sq.ft | 4.1% |

## 7. Eco-sensitive specifications

**Construction & envelope**
- Helical steel screw-pile foundation — zero concrete pour, root preservation
- 450 mm underfloor ventilation clearance
- Insulated walls (50 mm PIR + bamboo-batten rainscreen) and insulated roof (75 mm PIR)
- Lime-plaster finishes, charred-timber feature wall on the entry elevation
- Marine-grade epoxy coating on container shells for monsoon humidity resistance
- Low-VOC paints, water-based oiled-timber sealers

**Roof**
- Solar-ready: structural capacity for 5–7 kWp PV array, conduits and inverter wall pre-engineered
- Heat-reflective standing-seam metal, high-SRI coating
- Single-slope routing engineered to RWH tank at NE corner
- 900–1500 mm shaded overhangs plus secondary pergola layer over verandah

**Water**
- 15,000 L rainwater harvesting sump with multi-stage filtration
- Single groundwater recharge pit (sized to plot percolation)
- Greywater reed-bed filter pond feeding drip-irrigated herbal garden
- Drip-irrigation grid serving the plantation, orchard, and food forest
- Low-flow dual-flush WCs, aerated taps, greywater-fed urinal

**Energy**
- Solar-ready DC + AC architecture, inverter wall, net-meter provision
- LED throughout (3000 K interior / 2700 K outdoor)
- Cross-ventilation + courtyard stack-effect cooling
- Inverter + battery bank provision (5 kWh starter, scalable)
- Energy-efficient low-E argon-filled DGU on E/W/S, single-glazed on shaded N

**Landscape**
- Native canopy buffer along plot boundary (privacy + biodiversity)
- Cashew / mango / coconut / spice plantation per buyer profile
- Herbal + medicinal garden adjacent to kitchen
- Fruit-orchard accent zone (Alphonso / Mankurad / Hilsa)
- Butterfly + bird-friendly flowering native belt
- Permeable laterite + gravel walking paths — zero impermeable surface in plantation
- Composting twin-chamber at utility yard; anaerobic bio-septic with planted leach field
